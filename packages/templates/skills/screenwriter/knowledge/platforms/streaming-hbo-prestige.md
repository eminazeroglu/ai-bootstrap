# Streaming (HBO Prestige) — Platform

> Premium prestige content. HBO, MAX, Apple TV+ (premium side), AMC+, FX. Slow-burn character-driven storytelling.

## Defining characteristics

- "Prestige" branding (artistic intent)
- Slower pace than commercial streaming
- Auteur-driven (director/writer voice)
- Limited episodes per season (6-10)
- Higher per-episode budget
- Critical acclaim focus
- Complex characters

## Format conventions

### Episode length
- 50-80 minutes typical
- Some up to 90 min (treated as mini-features)
- Pilot can be feature-length

### Pacing
- Slower than commercial streaming
- More silence, breath
- Long takes welcome
- Character focus over plot

### Tone
- Adult, complex, often dark
- Moral ambiguity
- Anti-hero protagonists common
- Themes over plot

## Writing for prestige

### Character first
- Plot serves character revelation
- Internal world prioritized
- Every character has interiority

### Subtext heavy
- Dialogue meanings layered
- What's NOT said matters
- Patient audience

### Visual storytelling
- Trust the image
- Long takes
- Silence as language
- Cinematic compositions

### Theme integration
- Story has something to say
- Themes woven through
- Not didactic

## Examples of prestige

- The Wire — institutions, working class
- Succession — wealth, power, family
- White Lotus — class, privilege
- Fleabag — grief, intimacy
- Mad Men — ambition, identity
- Mare of Easttown — community, loss
- Breaking Bad — morality, ego
- Severance — work, identity

## Sample opening

```
COLD OPEN (3-7 min)
- May have no dialogue
- Atmospheric, character moment
- Sometimes seemingly unrelated to plot
- Mood/theme establishment

CREDITS (often elaborate)

ACT 1 (10-15 min)
- Multiple character introductions
- Slow build
- Subtle plot threads
```

## Content freedoms

- Full freedom (premium)
- Adult content
- Violence, sexuality, profanity
- Complex moral territory

Use freedom in service of theme, not gratuitously.

## Pacing comparison

| Element | Commercial streaming | HBO prestige |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| Hook timing | First 30s | First 5 min |
| Scene length | 2-4 min | 4-8 min |
| Dialogue density | High | Medium-low |
| Action density | Variable | Low |
| Resolution speed | Episode | Season |

## When to write for prestige

- Character-driven story
- Adult themes
- Auteur sensibility
- Critical aspirations
- Slower audience patience expected

## Differs from commercial streaming

Commercial streaming (Netflix typical) prioritizes:
- Hook, retention metrics
- Wider audience
- Faster pace

Prestige (HBO typical) prioritizes:
- Critical acclaim
- Artistic vision
- Slower deliberate pace
- Smaller dedicated audience

## Specific sub-styles

### British prestige
Slower still, more dialogue-driven (Fleabag, Black Mirror, Sherlock)

### Scandinavian prestige
Sparse, atmospheric (The Bridge, Borgen)

### Korean prestige
Visual-driven, melodrama elements (Kingdom, Squid Game)
