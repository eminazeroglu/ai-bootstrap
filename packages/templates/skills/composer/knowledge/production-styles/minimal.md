# Minimal — Production Style

> Sparse arrangement. Few elements. Each element matters. Less is more.

## Defining elements

- Few instruments at any time
- Space between elements
- Each element essential
- No filler
- Often slow tempo
- Restrained dynamics

## Sound character
- Spacious
- Intimate
- Contemplative
- Each sound audible
- "Empty" but full of meaning

## Genre fit
- Minimal techno
- Minimalist classical (Glass, Reich, Pärt)
- Modern composition (Einaudi, Richter)
- Indie folk (intimate)
- Singer-songwriter (just voice + guitar/piano)
- Ambient

## Production techniques
- Few tracks total
- Long sustained sounds
- Reverb for space
- Minimal processing
- Mono or simple stereo
- Quiet mastering (preserves dynamics)

## When to use
- Intimate songs
- Contemplative moments
- Avant-garde / art music
- Solo singer-songwriter
- Minimalist composition tradition

## Examples
- "The Sound of Silence" — Simon & Garfunkel
- Arvo Pärt "Spiegel im Spiegel" (just two instruments)
- Bon Iver early albums (sparse)
- Sufjan Stevens "Carrie & Lowell" (intimate)
- Steve Reich repetitive works

## Philosophy
"Less is more." Each element earns its place. Nothing extra.

## Avoid pairing with
- Maximalist genres (pop with everything)
- Stadium rock
- Orchestral fullness
- Heavy production layering
