# Hi-Fi — Production Style

> Maximum audiophile quality. Wide dynamic range. Clean, detailed, pristine.

## Defining elements

- High sample rate / bit depth (96kHz/24-bit+)
- Minimal compression
- Wide dynamic range
- Detailed transients
- Audiophile mastering
- Less loud (preserves dynamics)

## Sound character
- Pristine
- Detailed
- Spacious
- Three-dimensional
- Wide dynamic range
- Less fatiguing

## Genre fit
- Audiophile recordings
- Classical
- Acoustic jazz
- High-end pop / R&B (some)
- Vinyl-targeted releases
- Surround sound mixes

## Production techniques
- Minimal processing
- Light compression (transparent)
- Wide stereo image
- Natural reverb (real spaces)
- Mastered to higher dynamic range
- Vinyl-friendly (preserves dynamic range)

## When to use
- Audiophile market
- Classical / acoustic jazz
- Vinyl release planning
- Surround sound mixing
- Connoisseur audiences

## Famous hi-fi productions
- Steely Dan (legendarily detailed production)
- Donald Fagen "The Nightfly" (hi-fi reference)
- Various classical recordings (Decca, Deutsche Grammophon)
- Many audiophile labels (Telarc, Reference Recordings)
- Modern: Daft Punk "Random Access Memories" (vinyl-quality polish)

## Vs polished modern
- Polished modern = loud, compressed (modern radio)
- Hi-fi = detailed, dynamic (audiophile)

Different goals. Polished is for streaming/radio competition. Hi-fi is for sound quality appreciation.

## Avoid pairing with
- Lo-fi aesthetic (opposite)
- Loudness war mastering
- Heavy compression
- Bedroom production aesthetic
