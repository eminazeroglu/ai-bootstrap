# Melody Craft — Music Knowledge

> The art of melodic line. What makes a memorable, singable melody.

## What makes a good melody

### Memorability
- Hook that sticks after one listen
- Often based on simple intervals
- Rhythmic pattern recognizable

### Singability
- Within vocal range (typically 1.5 octaves max)
- Avoids extreme leaps (small steps preferred)
- Vowels on long notes
- Consonants on short notes

### Emotional truth
- Melody matches lyrical emotion
- Sad lyrics = descending lines often
- Happy lyrics = ascending or stable
- Tense moments = repeated notes or dissonance

### Shape
- Arc — rises and falls
- Climax — highest note often at chorus peak
- Resolution — returns to tonic at end

## Melodic intervals

### Steps (close together)
- Most common
- Singable
- Smooth feel

### Leaps (jumps)
- Use sparingly
- Create drama
- Resolve back to step

### Octave jumps
- Powerful moments
- Often at chorus
- Don't overuse

## Melodic patterns

### Sequence
Same shape, different starting note. Builds momentum.

### Repetition
Same notes. Drives home a phrase.

### Variation
Same idea, slightly altered. Keeps interest.

### Call and response
Two melodic phrases that "answer" each other.

## Verse vs chorus melody

### Verse
- Lower range usually
- More speech-like
- Rhythmically varied
- Allows lyrical variety

### Chorus
- Higher range typically
- More melodic (less speech)
- Anchored by hook phrase
- Repeats with same words

### Bridge
- Different pattern entirely
- Often modulates (key change)
- Builds to final chorus

## Genre-specific melody

### Pop
- Hook-driven
- Memorable chorus
- Often follows chord tones

### Folk
- Storytelling melody
- Subtle range
- Often modal

### Hip-hop
- Speech-based (rap)
- Hooks more melodic
- Auto-tune sometimes

### R&B
- Melismatic (many notes per syllable)
- Wide range
- Improvisational feel

### Rock
- Riff-based often
- Power notes
- Anthem-like choruses

## For AI music (Suno)

Suno generates melodies based on:
- Genre conventions
- Mood you specify
- BPM/key
- Vocal style requested

You don't write melody for Suno — but you specify what KIND of melody:
```
[Vocal: emotional alto, melismatic chorus, restrained verse]
```

## Common melody mistakes

- Out of singable range
- Too many leaps
- Forgettable chorus
- Same melody V1 and V2 (boring — vary)
- No clear hook
- Doesn't match lyrical emotion
