# Drill — Genre

> Aggressive hip-hop subgenre. Originated Chicago, spread to UK and Brooklyn. Dark, hard.

## Defining elements

- Aggressive trap-influenced beats
- Sliding 808 bass (signature)
- 60-75 BPM (slow but doubled feel for hi-hats)
- Dark atmosphere
- Tough, sometimes violent lyrical content
- Distinctive flow

## Sub-types

- **Chicago drill** — origin (Chief Keef, Lil Durk, Pop Smoke roots)
- **UK drill** — British (Headie One, Central Cee, Skepta)
- **Brooklyn drill** — NY (Pop Smoke, Sheff G, Fivio Foreign)
- **Latin drill** — emerging
- **French drill** — Booba, Niska

## Distinctive elements

- Sliding 808 (the iconic drill bass slide)
- Tight hi-hat patterns
- Sparse arrangement
- Reverb on vocals (UK drill especially)
- Often menacing piano / synth lead

## Typical instrumentation

808 (sliding) + drum machine + sparse synth/piano lead + vocal

## Production characteristics

- Dark atmosphere
- Sub-bass heavy
- Sparse (less is more)
- Reverb on vocals
- Tight programming

## Lyrics direction

- Street narratives
- Aggressive
- Specific local references (Chicago, London neighborhoods)
- Slang heavy (region-specific)
- Often provocative
- Pop Smoke lighter side: party, women, success

## Examples

- Chicago drill: Chief Keef, Lil Durk
- Brooklyn drill: Pop Smoke (defining figure), Sheff G, Fivio Foreign
- UK drill: Headie One, Central Cee, Digga D, Stormzy

## Avoid

- Bright major key
- Live drums
- Acoustic feel
- Soft vocals
- Generic hip-hop (drill has specific elements)
