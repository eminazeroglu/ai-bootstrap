# Azerbaijani Pop — Genre 🇦🇿

> Modern Azerbaijani popular music. Western pop production with Azerbaijani vocals + cultural elements.

## Defining elements

- Western pop production foundation
- Azerbaijani language vocals
- Often hybrid: pop + traditional elements
- Modern radio-friendly
- Star vocalists
- Mid tempo (95-120 BPM)

## Eras

### Soviet era pop
- Rashid Behbudov (legendary)
- Bul-bul-ogli
- State-supported pop

### Post-Soviet 1990s-2000s
- Aygün Kazımova (icon)
- Brilliant Dadaşova
- Faik Agayev

### Modern (2010s+)
- Eldar Qasımov (Eurovision winner 2011)
- Sabina Babayeva
- Nigar Camal
- Modern singer-songwriters

## Typical instrumentation

Drum machine + synth pads + bass + electric piano + occasional traditional instruments (tar, kamancha) + vocal + harmonies

## Production characteristics

- Modern polish
- Vocal forward
- Sometimes incorporates traditional instruments for cultural touch
- Russian / Turkish production influences
- Hybrid East-West

## Vocal style

- Often emotional / melismatic
- Influenced by mugham tradition (ornaments)
- Wide range expected
- Both genders prominent

## Lyrics direction

- Love (passionate)
- Heartbreak
- Cultural pride
- Modern life
- Specific Azerbaijani references (places, foods, traditions)
- Often poetic

## Examples

- **Aygün Kazımova** — pop icon, multiple decades
- **Brilliant Dadaşova** — classic Azerbaijani pop
- **Roya Ayxan** — international success
- **Eldar Qasımov + Nigar Camal** — Eurovision 2011 winners
- **Sevda Yahyayeva** — modern pop
- **Aygün Beyler** — singer-songwriter
- **Tofiq Quliyev** — composer (classic)

## Sub-types

### Pop ballad
- Slower, emotional
- Often about love

### Pop dance
- Faster, danceable
- Modern Eurovision style

### Pop folk fusion
- Pop with traditional instruments
- Cultural pride emphasized

### Pop romantic
- Duets common
- Wedding music adjacent

## Avoid

- Generic Western pop (loses cultural identity)
- Pure mugham (too traditional for pop)
- English-only lyrics (Azerbaijani primary)
- Heavy distortion
- Aggressive production
